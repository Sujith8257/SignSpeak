Place your hand in front of the webcam and make ISL gestures. SignSpeak combines a landmark-based Keras model and a RandomForest skeleton model to pick the most confident prediction for every frame.
• Real-time inference using MediaPipe hand landmarks
• Temporal smoothing for stable characters and spaces
• Optional text-to-speech for the full translated sentence

Built for accessibility and ease of use

Everything runs in your browser with a simple Flask backend and live WebSocket updates.

Real-time recognition

Stream webcam frames to the backend using an MJPEG feed and receive predictions via Socket.IO with minimal latency, even on CPU-only machines.

One or two hands

MediaPipe detects up to two hands. Features from both are combined so the models can understand single-hand and two-hand ISL gestures, including a palm gesture for space.

From text to speech

The web client collects recognized characters into words and sentences, then uses the browser’s speech synthesis to read them aloud with adjustable playback speed.

Indian Sign Language dataset – all 35 classes

SignSpeak is trained on an Indian Sign Language dataset with 35 classes covering digits 1–9 and letters A–Z. Each class contains multiple examples captured under consistent lighting and background conditions, enabling robust, real-time recognition.
